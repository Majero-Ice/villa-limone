# Villa Limone - Local Activities & Recommendations

## Things to Do in the Area

### Cinque Terre (20 minutes)

The famous Cinque Terre is a UNESCO World Heritage site consisting of five picturesque coastal villages: Monterosso, Vernazza, Corniglia, Manarola, and Riomaggiore.

**Best ways to explore:**
- Train: Regular trains connect all five villages (€5-10 per journey)
- Boat: Scenic boat tours available March-October (€25-35 per person)
- Hiking: Famous coastal trails between villages (free, trail pass required €7.50/day)

**Our recommendations:**
- Visit Vernazza at sunset for stunning views
- Try the local pesto in Monterosso
- Hike the Via dell'Amore (Lover's Walk) between Riomaggiore and Manarola
- Best time to visit: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds

### Portofino (15 minutes)

Charming fishing village turned luxury destination, famous for its colorful buildings and designer boutiques.

**Must-see:**
- Castello Brown (medieval castle with panoramic views)
- Church of San Giorgio
- Lighthouse walk (45-minute scenic trail)
- Piazzetta (main square) for people-watching and aperitivo

**Dining recommendation:**
- Try focaccia col formaggio at any local bakery
- Lunch at waterfront restaurants (book ahead in summer)

### Camogli (10 minutes)

Authentic Italian fishing village with colorful buildings and excellent seafood restaurants.

**Highlights:**
- Weekly market (Wednesday mornings)
- Pebble beach perfect for swimming
- Basilica of Santa Maria Assunta
- Fresh fish market at the harbor (mornings)
- Gelato at Bar Primula

### San Fruttuoso Abbey (30 minutes by boat)

Medieval abbey accessible only by boat or hiking trail, featuring the famous underwater Christ of the Abyss statue.

**Information:**
- Boat from Camogli: €15 round trip
- Abbey entrance: €8
- Snorkeling to see Christ statue: Equipment rental available
- Open April-October
